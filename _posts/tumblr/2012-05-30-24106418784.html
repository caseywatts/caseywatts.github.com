--- layout: post title: IBS = Jessica The Cranky Tummy /Explanation/ ---

I have this weird science diet I have to follow so I don’t have bellyaches all of the time. I used to get weekly/bi-weekly bellyaches that made me curl up and lose whole days. So much that we even named my belly “Jessica” 6 years ago for being such a bitch - but no more! Here’s the sitch:

IBS and Low-FODMAPs Diet. I have Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS) which is an ill-defined digestive disorder which we have in my family. To treat it, I follow the “Low-FODMAPs Diet” (low Fermentable Oligo/Di/Mono-saccharides And Polyols) by avoiding a long list of chemically related food. The low-FODMAP diet is considered the most effective current treatment of IBS by the scientific community, and my gastroenterologist prescribed it to me. Science doesn’t really know why I’m susceptible to FODMAPs and other people are just fine, but we do know how this diet does its magic.

It’s Science. Human and rat studies have shown that this set of food (FODMAPs) isn’t digested well by us mammals. And since we can’t digest it, it’s instead digested by greedy gut bacteria which go on to make acid and gas and shit :< But the symptoms of IBS can subside when we starve those naughty bacteria. By not eating this set of food, I can avoid the problem! (I can’t wait for the day where we understand the root cause and fix /that/ instead of just avoiding the problem. Science, full speed ahead!)

It’s Kinda Hard. Even though IBS is ill-defined and has many different causes, this diet is effective on so many people that it’s totally worth giving a shot! But it’s complicated and describes /so many/ food that are triggers that it’s difficult to do well. For two months trying to follow this diet I still had bellyaches just about as frequently as before - except I could point at exactly which food the triggers were once equipped with the FODMAP knowledge. And the learning process began.

But It’s Kinda Flexible! Now I understand what counts and doesn’t so well that I manage to successfully “cheat”. As long as I minimize transgressions I feel just fine. For instance, I recently had bechamel sauce made with a pinch of flour (which has a nasty FODMAP in it - fructans), and it didn’t even hurt me ^_~
