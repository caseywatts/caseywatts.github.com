--- layout: post title: Saving and Sharing Email Templates on GMail ---

There are situations where you want to share email templates with other people. It’s a simple idea, but it’s actually so hard to do. In client applications like Thunderbird and OS X’s Mail.app templates work well enough, but there’s absolutely no sharing. In GMail, “Canned Responses” is a lab feature that can store stock emails, but there’s no sharing there either.

But I just want to share a few small text fields! Just To, Subject, Body, CC, and BCC.

Here’s a javascript bookmarklet that does just that.

Copy this text and paste it into a bookmark.

javascript:prompt("Copy and paste this 'mailto' link into a bookmark to save this email template.", ('mailto:' + encodeURIComponent(document.getElementsByName('to')[0].value) + '?' + 'CC=' + encodeURIComponent(document.getElementsByName('cc')[0].value) + '&BCC=' + encodeURIComponent(document.getElementsByName('bcc')[0].value) + '&SUBJECT=' + encodeURIComponent(document.getElementsByName('subject')[0].value) + '&BODY=' + encodeURIComponent(document.getElementsByName('body')[0].value)))

To use it:

  1. Navigate to a gmail “compose” page and write your email.
  2. Press “Save”
  3. Click the bookmarklet, and a “mailto” link is generated.
  4. Copy-paste this “mailto” link into a bookmark.
  5. Share that bookmark!

Limitations:

  1. It only works with “plain text” emails. An HTML email just can’t fit in a bookmark.
  2. You have to press “save” before using the bookmarklet. (the save button puts the body into the parent frame in gmail where it’s easier to access and more reliably correct. Boomerang for GMail requires the save button be pressed too.)
